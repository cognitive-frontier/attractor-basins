The Socion as Attractor Landscape

Measured personality is continuous. Trait scores, function profiles, and similarity vectors all arrive as graded quantities, and an honest model starts from that substrate.●●● The typological claim sits one level up: a grammar of constraints (dichotomy potentials, axis laws, precision weightings, intertype couplings) carves recurring low-energy regions into the continuum.

A sociotype, on this reading, is an attractor basin: a self-reinforcing configuration that a person's generative model keeps returning to because expected surprise is lowest there. Basins carry depth (stability), width (tolerance for perturbation), and shape (the pattern of convergence), which is why the same framework covers both the decade-scale stability of trait scores and the genuine transitions that follow crisis or sustained intervention.●●○

The discrete reading works the way Bohr's orbitals work for chemistry: an idealization of a continuous substrate that stays the right tool at the scale where prediction happens. Trait distributions can remain bell-shaped while the configurations they parameterize settle into discrete wells; the landscape is continuous and the attractors are discrete. Toggle Compare and the grammar relaxes to a bare gradient field. That picture is still truthful, yet it says nothing about stability or about the lawful paths between basins, and that gap is where the constraint set earns its keep.

Core dichotomies●●●

Jung-Augusta poles behave as potential differences: each pole pair sets a gradient the flow respects. The macro walls here realize Merry/Serious (west to east) and Judicious/Decisive (north to south), with Extravert/Introvert and club partitions nested inside each quadra. The plane itself is a projection of dichotomy space, so the walls are coordinates made visible.

Information axes●●○

Complementary pairs (Ne–Si, Ni–Se, Ti–Fe, Te–Fi) bind quadra members into shared valleys, following the semiotic reading in which information circulates across the rational-irrational pairing and stalls within it. Contrary and conflicting element relations surface as the central massif and the super-ego barriers ringed around it; reaching a conflictor means paying the full crossing cost, which is why conflict pairs sit antipodal on this projection.

Model A precision●●○

Precision-weighted elements skew each basin toward its valued axis, and dimensional structure appears as ledges: shallow shelves where lower-dimensional processing still holds before the well proper begins. This is the Presence Cube read as terrain.

Hybrid elements●●○

Where two neighbors share an ego channel, a metastable saddle sits in the wall between them. Kindred pairs share the base element, lookalike pairs the creative; mirage and semi-dual pairs share an Integrated Element, the cross-order aspect and axis coordinates written N? through F!. Under stress the saddle's precision drops and the pass opens, so transitions route through shared machinery rather than across arbitrary walls.

Higher structures●○○

Quadra circuits, duality phase coupling, and conflictor avoidance rendered as weak force fields over the swarm. Directionally motivated by intertype theory and the group structure beneath it; quantitatively heuristic.

Each particle is a person-level trajectory carrying the three PersDyn parameters (Sosnowska, Hofmans, and De Fruyt, 2019): baseline location, intra-individual variability, and attractor strength, the pull that returns deviations to baseline. The sliders map onto these directly.●●○

Free energy reads as height. Basins are low-surprise regions, ridges are costly states, and the surprise index tracks the swarm's mean height above the field minimum. Stress raises noise while lowering barrier precision, so hybrid passes open and transition probability climbs.

Click the terrain to perturb locally. A settled particle spirals home; an over-perturbed one crosses a pass and re-settles elsewhere. That difference between return and transition is the practical payoff of drawing basins at all, since a bare trait field predicts neither.

Profile presets deform basin depths through a similarity vector over the sixteen types. The ILE-proximal preset is illustrative, shaped by ego-element overlap in the spirit of Talanov function-profile correlations rather than lifted from a specific dataset. Paste sixteen values between 0 and 1 in the listed order to ground the field in real scores.

The layout is a 2D projection chosen to preserve quadra adjacency and antipodal conflict. The full dichotomy space is higher-dimensional, so a few relations (semi-duality, benefit) read as longer paths than their true metric distance. DCNH acts as a field accent over the whole swarm, following the Gulenko energy-distribution reading of the four subtypes.●○○
